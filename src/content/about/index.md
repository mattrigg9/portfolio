There are so many skilled programmers out in the world, but only a fraction truly appreciate the delicate relationship that a user has with their machine. This relationship fascinates me. 

A good interface should anticipate a users need before they even need to think about it, offering an experience that feels magical.

Think about your favorite app. Why do you like it? Is it the little bursts of delight it gives you as you do something good?

Working in the tech industry since I was 14, I've seen the evolution of what we deem is an acceptable user experience. 

My name is Matt Rigg, and I create applications for humans.
